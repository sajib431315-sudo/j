What is Light?

Definition: Light is a form of energy that produces the sensation of vision. It travels in straight lines (rectilinear propagation) at approximately 3 × 10⁸ m/s in vacuum.

Refraction of Light

1 What is Refraction?

The phenomenon of bending of light as it passes obliquely from one optical medium to another is called refraction.

Cause of Refraction

The change in speed of light when crossing the boundary between two media causes the light ray to bend.

→
Rarer to Denser: Light bends towards the normal (speed decreases)
→
Denser to Rarer: Light bends away from the normal (speed increases)

Snell's Law of Refraction

μ = sin i / sin r = c / v

The ratio of sine of angle of incidence to sine of angle of refraction is constant for a given pair of media.

Real Depth vs Apparent Depth

Real Depth

The actual position of the bottom or a point inside water.

Apparent Depth

The position where the bottom appears to be when viewed from outside due to refraction.

μ = Real Depth / Apparent Depth
Since μ > 1 for water, apparent depth is always less than real depth.
⚠️ A pond appears shallower than it actually is
⚠️ A stick immersed in water appears bent
⚠️ Fishermen aim below the apparent position of fish

Total Internal Reflection (TIR)

1️⃣

Condition 1

Light must travel from denser to rarer medium

2️⃣

Condition 2

Angle of incidence must be greater than critical angle

💡

Result

Light reflects back completely into the same medium

Critical Angle

The angle of incidence in a denser medium for which the corresponding angle of refraction in the rarer medium is 90°.

Water → Air 48.6°
Glass → Air 42°
Diamond → Air 24°

Why diamond sparkles: Low critical angle (24°) means most light undergoes TIR, creating brilliant sparkle!

Optical Fibers & Applications

Structure of Optical Fiber

Core
Innermost layer, high refractive index
Cladding
Lower refractive index, enables TIR
Jacket
Protective outer covering
🏥

Endoscopy

Medical examination of internal organs using flexible light pipes. Used for stomach (endoscopy) and colon (colonoscopy) examination.

📡

Telecommunications

High-speed data transmission over long distances with minimal loss. Used for internet, telephone, and cable TV.

🔬

Keyhole Surgery

Laparoscopic surgery using modified light pipes. Smaller cuts, less pain, faster recovery.

🏜️ Mirage

Optical illusion of water on hot roads due to TIR in air layers of different temperatures.

💎 Diamond Sparkle

Multiple TIR inside diamond due to low critical angle (24°) creates brilliant sparkle.

Different colors have different wavelengths. Violet light (shortest λ) bends the most, while red light (longest λ) bends the least.

Myopia (Short-sightedness)

Can see near objects clearly, but not distant objects.

Cause: Eyeball too long Correction: Concave lens
🔍

Hypermetropia (Long-sightedness)

Can see distant objects clearly, but not near objects.

Cause: Eyeball too short Correction: Convex lens
👴

Presbyopia

Age-related loss of accommodation ability.

Cause: Weakening of ciliary muscles Correction: Bifocal lens

Summary

Refraction

  • • Bending of light at media boundaries
  • • Snell's Law: μ = sin i / sin r
  • • Real depth > Apparent depth
  • • Causes: mirage, twinkling stars

Total Internal Reflection

  • • Denser to rarer medium
  • • Angle > Critical angle
  • • Applications: Optical fibers
  • • Endoscopy, keyhole surgery

Lenses & Eye

  • • Convex: Converging, real focus
  • • Concave: Diverging, virtual focus
  • • Power P = 1/f (Dioptre)
  • • Myopia: Concave, Hypermetropia: Convex

Key Formulas

μ = c/v = Real depth/Apparent depth
sin C = 1/μ (Critical angle)
1/f = 1/v - 1/u
P = 1/f (Dioptre)
